Bioinformatics

Bioinformatics sits at the intersection of biology, computer science, and statistics. It provides the computational framework for analyzing genomic data, modeling biological networks, and extracting meaningful patterns from large-scale datasets.

This section documents notes on tools, algorithms, and workflows I use in my research — including pipeline development, statistical methods for differential expression analysis, and genome assembly techniques.

More notes will be added as research progresses.
