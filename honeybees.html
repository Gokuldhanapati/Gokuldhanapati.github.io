Honey Bees

Honey bees (Apis mellifera) are among the most studied social insects, serving as critical pollinators for both wild ecosystems and agricultural systems. Their complex colonial organization, communication through dance language, and sophisticated division of labor make them a fascinating model for studying social behavior at the molecular level.

This page serves as a collection of notes and observations from my ongoing research into honey bee biology. Topics covered include colony dynamics, foraging behavior, and the genetic basis of caste determination.

More notes will be added as research progresses.
